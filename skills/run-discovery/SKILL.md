# run-discovery

The product owner does not assume it is right; it reduces risk with evidence.
Given a problem statement or a load-bearing assumption, produce a discovery plan
and commission it. You typically do not run the research yourself.

## Steps

1. **Name the assumption that must be true** for the initiative to make sense,
   and the cost of being wrong. If several, rank by "which, if false, kills it."
2. **Pick the method to the question.** Qualitative (interviews, observation)
   answers WHY; quantitative (data pull, survey, experiment) answers WHAT and
   HOW MANY. Triangulate when the stakes justify it.
3. **Write the specific questions to answer**, not vague themes. For interviews,
   the golden rule: ask about PAST behaviour ("tell me about the last time you
   tried to solve this"), never hypothetical future ("would you use this?").
4. **Commission it.** Hand the plan to the data-analyst (for a data pull) or a
   research spawn (for interviews), with the exact questions and the decision
   the answer will inform.
5. **Set the bar.** State what result would validate vs kill the assumption,
   before the data comes back, so the answer cannot be rationalised after.

## Output

A discovery plan: the assumption, the method, the specific questions, who runs
it, and the pre-committed decision rule.
