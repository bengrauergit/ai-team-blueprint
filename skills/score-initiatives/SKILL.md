# score-initiatives

Prioritisation needs a defensible reason, not a vibe. Pick the framework that
fits the org's maturity and the data you actually have, apply it, and show the
working. You may run two and compare.

## Pick the framework to the situation

- **Small / pre-PMF / thin data (the common default):** JTBD problem strength,
  a value-vs-effort read, and hand-counted funnel evidence. RICE "reach" and A/B
  significance are NOISE at a tiny base; do not fake them.
- **Enough initiatives and data to score:** RICE (reach, impact, confidence,
  effort), ICE, MoSCoW for release cuts, weighted scoring when the criteria must
  be explicit and defensible.
- **Agile at scale:** WSJF (value + time-criticality + risk-reduction over job
  size).
- **Feature-type calls:** Kano (must-have / performance / delighter), opportunity
  scoring (Ulwick, ties to JTBD).

## Steps

1. Name the candidates and the strategic goal they are ranked against.
2. Choose the framework and say why it fits (scale, data, decision type).
3. Score each candidate, showing the inputs, not just the total.
4. Rank, and include "do nothing" and "do the cheap boring thing" as real
   entries.
5. **The override:** if the data cannot support the framework, say so and drop
   to the honest read. A precise score on invented inputs is worse than an
   honest rough one.

## Output

A ranked list with the framework named, the inputs shown, and the cut line drawn.
